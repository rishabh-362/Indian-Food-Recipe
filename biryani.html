Vegetable Biryani Recipe

Prep in: 20m

Cooks in: 40m

Total in: 1h

Makes: 1 Serve

Ingredients

  • 1/3 cup and 1 tablespoon basmati rice
  • 1/2 large onion
  • 1 and 1/2 clove
  • nutmeg as required
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic paste
  • 1 and 1/2 tablespoon potato
  • 1 and 1/2 tablespoon bean green
  • salt As required
  • 1 and 1/2 tablespoon yoghurt (curd)
  • 3/4 green cardamom
  • 1/2 sprigs mint leaves
  • kewra as required
  • 1 and 1/2 cup and 1 and 1/2 tablespoon water
  • 1 and 1/2 tablespoon ghee
  • 1/4 teaspoon black cumin seeds
  • 1/2 cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon ginger paste
  • 1 and 1/2 tablespoon peas
  • 1 and 1/2 tablespoon cauliflower
  • 1 and 1/2 tablespoon carrot
  • black pepper as required
  • 3/4 black cardamom
  • 1/2 bay leaf
  • rose water as required
  • 1/2 tablespoon unsalted butter

How to make Vegetable Biryani

Step 1 Soak rice for half an hour

Wash the rice under cold running water and soak it for half an hour.

Step 2 Fry the sliced onion

Heat a pan with 4 tablespoons of ghee over moderate flame. Fry the sliced onions till golden brown and remove with a slotted spoon. Drain fried slices of onion on absorbent paper.

Step 3 Saute the whole spices

In the same pan, saute half of the black cumin seeds till they begin to sizzle. Add cloves, cinnamon, half of the nutmeg, peppercorns and saute them till they get fragrant.

Step 4 Saute the vegetables with yoghurt

Add ginger and garlic paste and saute for one minute. Now add salt, pepper, butter, yoghurt and vegetables and saute them over low heat till the vegetables are almost tender.

Step 5 Boil water with salt

Meanwhile, in a separate large pan, bring 8 cups of water to a boil with 2 teaspoons of salt.

Step 6 Make a bundle of whole spices

Tie the remaining cloves, cinnamon stick, cumin seeds, black cardamoms and green cardamoms in a piece of muslin cloth to make a small bundle (potli) and add to the water with bay leaves. Cook on low heat for 15-20 minutes to allow the spices to infuse the water with their flavour.

Step 7 Cook rice in the above water

Drain the rice, add to the pan and cook till they are half done. Remove from the heat and reserve the strained water.

Step 8 Assemble the vegetable biryani and serve hot

Stir the remaining ghee into the rice and set aside. To assemble the biryani, spread half the fried onions at the base of a deep heavy-bottomed heatproof casserole. Spread half the rice over the onions. Then spread a layer of the vegetables and the chopped mint over the rice. For garnishing, sprinkle rose water over the rice. Serve hot with raita.

Shahi Veg Biryani with Kofta Curry Recipe

Prep in: 50m

Cooks in: 100m

Total in: 2h 30m

Makes: 1 Serve

Ingredients

  • 1/3 cup basmati rice
  • 1/2 tablespoon gram flour (besan)
  • 1 and 1/4 clove
  • 1/4 beetroot
  • salt As required
  • 1 and 1/4 tablespoon potato
  • 2/3 Large tomato
  • 3/4 leaves bay leaf
  • 21 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon sugar
  • 1/4 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 and 1/3 cup water
  • 2 teaspoon raisins
  • 2 teaspoon cashews
  • 2 and 3/4 tablespoon soybean
  • 1/4 red chilli
  • 1 green chillies
  • 1/3 teaspoon ginger paste
  • 1/4 bunch coriander leaves
  • 2 and 3/4 tablespoon mustard oil
  • 2/3 tablespoon ghee
  • 1/4 teaspoon black cardamon
  • 1/3 Large capsicum
  • 1/3 cup peas
  • 1/2 tablespoon turmeric
  • 1/3 cup green beans
  • 1 and 1/2 peppercorns
  • 1 and 1/4 cinnamon stick
  • 2 and 3/4 tablespoon paneer

How to make Shahi Veg Biryani with Kofta Curry

Step 1

To prepare this delicious biryani recipe, first prepare the kofta. For the same, boil the green raw bananas and the potatoes till done and allow them cool naturally. Once done, peel and mash them in a bowl and add salt for taste.

Step 2

Next, to the bowl of mashed banana and potatoes, add besan (2 tablespoons), paneer (50 gms), turmeric powder (1/4 teaspoon), tomato (1 sliced), green chilli (1 small), ginger paste (1/2 teaspoon), coriander powder (1/4 teaspoon), green coriander (2 stems, cut into pieces), crushed black cardamom (1/2 teaspoon), salt (according to taste) along with water (as required). Mix well all the ingredients to make a smooth dough like consistency.

Step 3

Divide this mixture into small parts and shape them into smooth medium balls. Now, put a kadhai over medium flame and heat oil in it. To this oil, add 2 tablespoons of ghee. Once this ghee and oil mixture is hot enough, add these balls and deep fry them. Turnover the koftas and fry them till they turn brown in colour. Now, you take out the koftas in a plate and follow the same method for frying rest of the koftas. Your banana kofta is ready for biryani and curry.

Step 4

For kofta curry, put the mustard or refined oil (1/2 cup) and ghee (1 tablespoon) into a large pan. Then, add red chilli (1), green chilli (3), bay leaf (2), crushed black cardamom (1/2 teaspoon) in the pan and let them crackle. Next, add and fry carrot (1/2 cup, sliced), bean (1 cup), beetroot (1, small & sliced), peas (1 cup), tomato (2), salt (according to taste), turmeric powder (1/4 teaspoon), capsicum (1) and coriander powder (4 teaspoon). Stir and mix well, and fry all these vegetables for about 10 minutes.

Step 5

Now, put 1-2 cup water (according to taste) into the mixed masala when the masala starts boiling, boil again for 2 minutes. Put (the garam masala) cinnamon stick (3), peppercorns (4), cumin seed (1/2 small spoon), ginger paste (1 teaspoon), clove (3), green coriander (3 stems, cut into pieces), sugar, cashew nuts (2 teaspoon) and koftas (1 medium bowl) into the curry. Lightly stir and mix all the ingredients properly. Turn off the gas knob and the kofta will take 5 minutes to be hot. Now take out the recipe in a large bowl. You can garnish it with green coriander (1 sliced) & raisins. Your banana kofta is ready to serve. (Note: You can eat 'Kofta Curry' with 'Shahi Veg Biryani', rice, bread, roti and chapati.)

Step 6

The next step is to prepare biryani. For the same, soak the soybeans in enough water for 15-20 minutes. Then, boil these soybeans until they become soft. Drain the water and keep them aside. Afterwards, make a fine paste of clove (2), peppercorns (2), coriander powder, cinnamon stick, black cardamon and green chilli by grinding them in a grinder jar.

Step 7

Now, cut paneer (100 gms) into small pieces in a bowl and keep aside. Then, put a kadhai over medium flame and add enough oil in it or shallow frying. Once the oil is hot enough, add the paneer pieces and fry them. Once done, keep aside until required. Put a large bowl on medium flame and add rice in it along with enough water to boil. Then, add the prepared spice paste in the rice to enhance the flavour and sprinkle a little salt as per your taste and ghee (1 tablespoon). Once the rice is done, keep aside for later use.

Step 8

Now, take a deep vessel and heat ghee(1 tablespoon) and refined oil (1/2 cup) in it. Once the oil-ghee mixture is hot enough, add all the remaining spices and let them crackle for a minute. Then, add finely chopped tomato (1), carrot (1), beans (1 cup), beetroot (1/2 cup) in the vessel along with peas (1 cup), green chilli (2 small), salt (1 teaspoon). Saute these veggies for about a minute or two and then add ginger paste in it along with sliced capsicum. Stir fry for 2-3 minutes.

Step 9

Finally, add the prepared koftas (2 cup) in this vessel along with, red chili, coriander powder (1/2 teaspoon), crushed black cardamom (1/2 teaspoon), cinnamon stick (4), clove (3), peppercorns (3), cashew nuts and raisin (1/2 cup), bay leaf (2), sugar (1/2 teaspoon), paneer (50-100 gms). Cook the mixture till the mixture leaves oil on the sides of the vessel.

Step 10

Now, add the boiled soybean chunks and let the mixture cook for another 5 minutes. Finally, add the boiled rice and mix with the mixture properly. You can add koftas after boiled rice also. Allow the biryani to cook for five minutes. Garnish the biryani with kesar (saffron) and veg salad. Your mix veg biryani is ready to eat. Serve it with kofta curry or boondi raita.

Hydrabadi Gobhi Mussalam Biryani Recipe

Prep in: 10m

Cooks in: 1h

Total in: 1h 10m

Makes: 1 Serve

Ingredients

  • 3/4 small cauliflower
  • 1/4 tablespoon ginger paste
  • 1/2 cup basmati rice
  • 3 teaspoon milk
  • 1/4 inch cinnamon stick
  • 2 clove
  • 2 tablespoon onion
  • salt As required
  • 1/4 teaspoon turmeric
  • 3 mint leaves
  • 1/4 cup all purpose flour
  • 1/4 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1/4 tablespoon garlic paste
  • 1/4 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 3 and 3/4 strand saffron
  • 1 and 1/4 green cardamom
  • 1/4 teaspoon black cumin seeds
  • 2 and 1/2 peppercorns
  • 3 tablespoon hung curd
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chilli powder
  • 1/2 tablespoon ghee
  • 1/2 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • water As required

For Garnishing

  • 1/4 sprig coriander leaves

Hydrabadi Gobhi Mussalam Biryani Recipe

Step 1

Wash the cauliflowers properly and remove the stem. In a mortar and pestle pound together the ginger and garlic. Now, in a separate bowl, add lemon juice along with the ginger garlic paste and stir well. Marinate these cauliflower florets in this bowl and set aside.

Step 2

Now, soak the basmati rice in water for sometime. Meanwhile, warm the milk on a pan over medium flame. Soak the saffron strands in it and keep it aside. Now, prepare the biryani masala by grinding green cardamoms, cinnamon stick, black cumin seeds, cloves and peppercorns together.

Step 3

Place a pan over medium flame and fry the chopped onion slices till they become dark brown in color. Add these fried onions to the marinated cauliflower florets. Also, add hung curd, green chili powder, salt, red chili powder, turmeric powder, coriander powder and ghee to the same bowl making sure the cauliflower florets have an even coating of this marination paste. Keep it aside for sometime.

Step 4

Now in a deep bottomed pan, pour water along with rice, mint leaves and salt over medium flame. Once it starts boiling, switch the gas flame to low and cook for not more than 5-7 minutes. Meanwhile, start kneading the dough by mixing flour and water together. Once you have a dough, keep it on the side.

Step 5

Now, take a deep bottomed biryani pot and place it over a medium flame. Add oil to it and once it's hot enough, put the marinated cauliflower florets in it. Let them cook for sometime. Meanwhile, heat a cup of water in a microwave for a minute and then pour this on top of it. Now, add some of the boiled rice to this pot. After sometime, add the left over rice as well.

Step 6

In the same pot, add some more oil along with the saffron milk prepared in the second step in a swirl so that it spreads evenly. Cover it with a lid and cook it on full flame. Seal this pot from all the sides using the dough prepared above. After sometime, touch the lid with the side of your fingers. If it's very hot, switch the flame to medium and then after 6-7 minutes turn it to low.

Step 7

After 10-15 minutes, take this pot off the heat. Your Hydrabadi Gobhi Mussalam Biryani is now ready. Open it only after 30 minutes and serve immediately. Don't forget to garnish it with coriander leaves on the top and enjoy.
