Khaman Dhokla Recipe

Prep in: 30m

Cooks in: 15m

Total in: 45m

Makes: 1 Serve

Ingredients

  • 1/2 cup gram flour (besan)
  • 1/2 teaspoon sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 tablespoon refined oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1/3 cup and 2 teaspoon water
  • 1/2 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 7 and 1/2 curry leaves

For Garnishing

  • 2 green chilli
  • 1/2 teaspoon coconut powder
  • 1/2 handful coriander leaves

How to make Khaman Dhokla

Step 1 Prepare the Dhokla batter and let it ferment for 1-2 hours.

To make this easy Khaman Dhokla recipe, take a large mixing bowl and add gram flour, salt, water, lemon juice and baking soda to it. Mix all these ingredients well. Allow the batter to ferment for 1-2 hours. If you are making it in the winter season, this might take longer. In the meantime, pour boiled water into a steamer and grease the utensil with oil.

Step 2 Cook the batter for 15-20 minutes in the idli/dhokla cooker.

Pour the dhokla batter in the greased utensil and cook on low flame in a steamer for 15-20 minutes. Check with a knife after 15 minutes by inserting it inside the dhokla. If the knife comes out clean, remove it from the stove. Allow the dish to cool and then cut into squares.

Step 3 Prepare the tempering.

For the tempering, heat a pan with oil in it over a medium flame. Once the oil is sufficiently hot, add mustard seeds, curry leaves and vertically sliced green chilli. Add 1/2 cup of water to the pan and allow it to boil. On 2-3 boils, lower the flame and squeeze 1/2 lemon, add sugar and green coriander leaves. If you prefer higher spice levels, you can also add some finely chopped green chillies. You can also make a Dhokla sandwich by layering Dhokla and adding your favourite sauce to it!

Step 4 Serve the moist Khaman Dhokla with Faafda and Jalebi

Turn off the heat and pour the tempering on the dhokla and sprinkle the coconut powder over the dish. Transfer the dish to a plate and serve it with any spicy chutney that you like such as green coriander chutney. Khaman Dhokla is best enjoyed when paired with Faafda and Jalebi. Try this delicious snack recipe at home with your family and friends.

Step 5

Take out the paneer curry in a bowl and garnish with coriander. Serve hot with chapati and rice.

Gujarati Muthiya Recipe

Prep in: 15m

Cooks in: 45m

Total in: 1h

Makes: 1 Serve

Ingredients

  • 1/4 cup chick peas
  • salt as required
  • 1/4 tablespoon coriander seeds
  • 1 and 3/4 cloves garlic
  • 1/4 tablespoon cumin seeds
  • refined oil as required
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1/4 teaspoon turmeric
  • 3/4 green chilli
  • 1/4 inch ginger
  • 2 tablespoon fenugreek leaves ( methi)

How to make Gujrati Muthiya

Step 1

To start with, soak chickpea in hot water for 2-3 hours or until they tenderize. Drain out water once done and grind chickpea, ginger, garlic, chilies, cumin and coriander in a grinder into a paste.

Step 2

Take a bowl and pour the paste in it. Add chopped fenugreek leaves, salt and curd in the bowl. Mix well.

Step 3

Take a kadai, keep it on medium flame and heat oil in it. Pour spoonful of the mixture in the kadai and deep fry it until golden brown in color.

Step 4

Take out on an absorbent paper and enjoy your crispy, hot muthiyas with your favourite sauce or chutney.

Gujarati Chivda Recipe

Prep in: 05m

Cooks in: 15m

Total in: 20m

Makes: 1 Serve

Ingredients

  • 1/2 cup brown rice poha
  • 1/4 handful green chilli
  • asafoetida as required
  • 1/4 teaspoon sugar
  • 1salt as required
  • mustard seeds as required
  • 1/4 handful neem leaves
  • turmeric as required
  • 1/4 teaspoon refined oil

For Garnishing

  • 1/3 tablespoon cashews
  • 1/3 tablespoon raisins

For The Main Dish

  • 1/3 tablespoon raw peanuts

How to make Gujarati Chivda

Step 1

Roast the poha and peanuts in a non-stick pan and make them crisp.

Step 2

In an another pan, heat oil and add the mustard seeds, asafoetida, green chillies, neem leaves and saute.

Step 3

When the seeds crackle, add the turmeric powder as well.

Step 4

Add the roasted mixture to the other pan and mix it with the green chillies and neem leaves.

Step 5

Now, add salt and mix well all the ingredients, and add sugar to it.

Step 6

If you are not high on calories then you can deep fry cashew nuts along with raisins and garnish this mixture

Step 7

Let it cool for a while and then store it in a box so that it remains crisp.

Step 8

Serve this chivda with masala chai or coffee

Gujarati Khandvi Recipe

Prep in: 05m

Cooks in: 20m

Total in: 25m

Makes: 1 Serve

Ingredients

  • 1/4 cup gram flour (besan)
  • 2 tablespoon water
  • 1/4 teaspoon turmeric
  • 1/4 teaspoon green chillies
  • 1/2 tablespoon refined oil
  • 1/4 cup yoghurt (curd)
  • salt As required
  • 1/4 teaspoon ginger paste
  • 1/2 tablespoon coriander leaves

For Tempering

  • 1/4 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1/2 stalks curry leaves

How to make Gujarati Khandvi

Step 1

In a bowl mix besan, dahi, water and make a batter.

Step 2

Now, add all spices in it like ginger paste, green chili, turmeric powder, salt and mix well.

Step 3

Now pour the batter in a steel bowl or flat vessel. Place the cooker on gas fill the cooker with water so that half of your steel vessel dips in it. .

Step 4

Now place the bowl in the cooker, close the lid and cook for 3 to 4 whistles.

Step 5

Now grease a plate with some oil. Take some batter and spread a thin layer on the plate. Let it cool down for 5-10 minutes.

Step 6

Garnish with coriander, cut into rectangular pieces and roll into khandvis. Repeat the process with the rest of the mixture.

Step 7

For the tempering, add a tbsp of oil in a pan, add mustard seeds and curry leaves. Let it crackle for a minute.

Step 8

Pour the tempering on the khandvis and serve.
